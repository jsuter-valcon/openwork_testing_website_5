Grants

Unique - Rare Chromosome Disorder Support Group

We provided funding of £10,000 to be used on their 'Genomic Jargon Buster' project, a new service for families using dynamic, digital content to de-mystify complex terminology, helping them better understand their child's diagnosis.

Visit Website

Oarsome Chance

We provided funding of £7,500 to cover the costs of small practical projects, a 'Project Pot', as a part of a programme of workshop and vocational learning.

Visit website

Hull Homeless Community Project

We provided funding of £10,500 for the 'move in project' which supports vulnerable adults and children back into safe and secure housing in the community after homelessness, abuse and crisis.

Visit website

TinyLife

We provided funding of £10,000 to partially fund the salary of the Family Support Co-ordinator, who provides early essential support and signposting for families experiencing premature birth and unwell babies.

Visit website

Northampton Saints Foundation

We provided funding of £10,000 to cover core salary costs of the Social Inclusion programmes which are expanding to meet increasing demand, driven by worsening mental health and behavioural issues seen in young people.

Viist website

1625 Independent People

We provided funding of £6,500 for the Participation and Learning Programme. The Programme creates a developmental ladder for vulnerable young people as they move towards increasing independence.

Visit website

Viyson

We provided funding of £15,000 to contribute to the core costs of delivering mental health and emotional wellbeing services for young people who are taking the first step to ask Visyon for support.

Visit website

Over the wall

We provided funding of £10,000 to plan and deliver our combined five day Health Challenge and Siblings Camp at St Mary's Ascot 3-7 April 2023 for children affected by serious illness in the UK.

Visit website

Big Breakfast Plus

We provided funding of £7,500 to support Big Breakfast Plus in delivering its core breakfast service to their guests.

Visit website

The Royal Alfred Seafarers' Society

We provided funding of £7,850 to install a sensory raised bed with water feature in the secure courtyard, used by our residents suffering with Dementia.
